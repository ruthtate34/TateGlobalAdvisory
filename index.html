About Ruth

A practitioner,
not just an advisor.

Founder & Principal Consultant

With over eight years in global HR — including three years focused on employee relations operations at the International Rescue Committee, a leading humanitarian organization — Ruth Tate has seen firsthand what happens when HR gets it right, and the cost when it doesn't.

At IRC, Ruth led operations within the Global Employee Relations function, working in close partnership with senior leadership to transform how the organization managed complex, cross-border workplace matters. She drove process improvements that reduced case backlogs and cut investigation timelines, and helped build capability programs that reached regional HR teams across more than 50 countries.

She founded Tate Global Advisory to bring that same rigorous, human-centered approach to mission-driven organizations everywhere.

How we work

Principled. Practical.
People-first.

Every engagement is built on three commitments — to your organization, your people, and the work itself.

01

Partnership over prescription

We don't arrive with a fixed playbook. We listen first, learn your context, and co-create solutions that fit your organization's reality — not a generic HR template.

02

Rigor with empathy

Complex employee matters require both analytical precision and genuine human care. We hold both — conducting thorough, impartial work while never losing sight of the people involved.

03

Lasting capability

Our goal is never dependency. We build internal capacity wherever we can, leaving your team more confident, better equipped, and less reliant on outside support.
